# Mental-Health-Monitoring-in-Social-Media-with-Deep-Learning-and-LLMs
Suicide continues to be a significant global public health concern, taking many
lives annually. The growth of social media platforms allows for real-time men
tal health monitoring, especially for the detection of depression. Its extensive
use offers a special chance to examine user-generated content for mental health
insights, offering a non-intrusive way to keep an eye on and assist those at risk.
Emojis, slang, and context-dependent expressions are examples of informal,
frequently ambiguous language used in social media that traditional depres
sion detection algorithms find difficult to interpret. This study examines how
Transformers-based models could transform suicide risk prediction by iden
tifying textual biomarkers in relevant data to overcome this constraint. The
effectiveness of many LLMs, including BERT, RoBERTA, DeBERTa, Distil
BERT, and Electra, in categorizing text material about suicide is the main
topic of the study
